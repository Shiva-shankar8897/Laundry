Ironing is the use of an iron, usually a heated iron(an iron), to remove wrinkles and unwanted creases from fabric.[1] The heating is commonly done to a temperature of 180–220 °Celsius (356-428 Fahrenheit), depending on the fabric.[2] Ironing works by loosening the bonds between the long-chain polymer molecules in the fibres of the material.
